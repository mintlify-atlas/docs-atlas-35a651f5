## Project Overview

UniJAC is a comprehensive community management platform designed to strengthen communication and participation among residents of Bogotá neighborhoods. The platform connects neighbors with their local Junta de Acción Comunal (JAC) and provides tools for reporting issues, organizing events, and building stronger communities.

## Mission Statement

In many neighborhoods across Bogotá, residents face daily challenges such as:
- Broken street lighting
- Garbage accumulation
- Excessive noise
- Lack of information about community activities

While these issues directly impact quality of life, there aren't always quick and organized channels to report them, track their progress, and receive clear responses from the Junta de Acción Comunal (JAC).

**UniJAC addresses this need** by providing a digital space that strengthens community communication and promotes more active citizen participation. More than just a technological tool, it creates a connection between residents and their representatives, facilitates incident management, and contributes to more transparent and efficient neighborhood administration.

## General Objective

To develop and implement a comprehensive web-based community management platform aimed at strengthening channels of communication, participation, and reporting of local issues among residents of Bogotá neighborhoods, through a centralized digital system administered by the Junta de Acción Comunal (JAC).

## Specific Objectives

### 1. Visual Design Development

Develop a visual model to facilitate the construction of a web page using Figma as the design tool, completed within a 1-week timeframe.

### 2. Technical Structure

Build a basic structure using HTML and CSS through applications like Visual Studio Code and GitHub to establish the functional and visual foundation of the web platform, ensuring:
- Clear navigation
- Proper content organization
- Correct display of community modules

Timeline: Two weeks

### 3. Digital Reporting System

Implement a digital reporting system that allows neighbors to communicate community problems and facilitates the JAC's ability to track and manage them.

Timeline: Three weeks

## Project Timeline

The development of UniJAC was completed in approximately **four weeks**, following an agile development approach:

- **Week 1**: Visual design and prototyping in Figma
- **Week 2**: HTML/CSS structure and basic layout
- **Week 3**: Digital reporting system implementation
- **Week 4**: Testing, refinement, and deployment
