## The Importance of Community Events

Community events are vital for building social connections, fostering civic participation, and creating a sense of belonging among neighborhood residents. As a JAC administrator, you have the tools to organize and promote events that bring the community together.

## Types of Community Events

UniJAC supports various event types that strengthen community bonds:

### Health & Wellness
- **Rumba terapia**: Community dance therapy sessions (as seen in the neighborhoods dashboard)
- **Vaccination drives**: Coordinated immunization campaigns
- **Health screenings**: Blood pressure checks, diabetes testing, etc.
- **Fitness activities**: Morning walks, yoga classes, aerobics

### Environmental
- **Cleanup campaigns**: Neighborhood beautification efforts
- **Tree planting**: Green space improvement initiatives
- **Recycling workshops**: Education on waste management
- **Environmental awareness**: Sustainability education sessions

### Social & Cultural
- **Cultural celebrations**: Holiday festivities and traditional events
- **Art workshops**: Painting, crafts, music classes
- **Movie nights**: Outdoor cinema for families
- **Book clubs**: Literary discussion groups

### Sports & Recreation
- **Sports tournaments**: Football, basketball, volleyball competitions
- **Marathon events**: Running and walking events
- **Swimming activities**: Pool events for families
- **Children's activities**: Organized play and learning sessions

### Civic Engagement
- **JAC meetings**: Community assemblies and planning sessions
- **Educational workshops**: Rights, responsibilities, and civic duties
- **Safety seminars**: Crime prevention and emergency preparedness
- **Municipal presentations**: Updates from local government officials

## Creating a New Event

Follow these steps to create and publish a community event:

### Step 1: Plan the Event Details

Before creating the event in UniJAC, ensure you have:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Event Concept">
    Define the event's purpose, target audience, and expected outcomes.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Date and Time">
    Select a date and time that works for most residents (weekends often have better attendance).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Location">
    Identify the venue (e.g., "Alcaldía del barrio", community center, park, school).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Resources Needed">
    List required materials, equipment, volunteers, and budget.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Permissions">
    Obtain necessary permits from local authorities if required.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Step 2: Create the Event in UniJAC

From your admin dashboard:

1. Navigate to the **Eventos** section using the left sidebar button
2. Click the "Create New Event" button
3. Fill in the event information form:

**Required Fields**:
- **Event Name**: Clear, descriptive title (e.g., "Rumba terapia")
- **Date**: Event date in DD/MM/YYYY format
- **Time**: Start time (e.g., "7:00 AM")
- **Location**: Venue name and address
- **Category**: Type of event (health, sports, cultural, etc.)
- **Description**: Detailed information about the event

**Optional Fields**:
- **End Time**: When the event concludes
- **Contact Person**: JAC member responsible for the event
- **Registration Required**: Whether residents need to sign up in advance
- **Capacity Limit**: Maximum number of participants
- **Cost**: If there's any participation fee
- **Image**: Event poster or promotional image

### Step 3: Review and Publish

1. Preview the event listing to ensure all information is accurate
2. Click "Publish" to make the event visible to the community
3. The event will appear in the "Eventos y actividades disponibles" section of the neighborhood dashboard

<Note>
Events are displayed as cards showing the title, location, and date/time. For example:

**Rumba terapia**  
Alcaldía del barrio  
14/03/2026 – 7:00 AM
</Note>

## Event Scheduling Best Practices

### Timing Considerations

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Weekend Events">
    Schedule most events on weekends when more residents are available. Saturday mornings (7:00 AM - 11:00 AM) work well for fitness activities.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Evening Events">
    Weekday evenings (6:00 PM - 8:00 PM) accommodate working residents for meetings or educational sessions.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Avoid Conflicts">
    Check the neighborhood schedule for garbage collection days and other regular activities to avoid conflicts.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Lead Time">
    Publish events at least 2 weeks in advance to give residents time to plan attendance.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Venue Selection

- **Alcaldía del barrio**: Suitable for official meetings and formal events
- **Community parks**: Ideal for outdoor activities, sports, and large gatherings
- **Schools**: Good for educational workshops and children's activities
- **Community centers**: Indoor events, classes, and social gatherings
- **Public squares**: Cultural celebrations and neighborhood-wide events

Ensure the venue:
- Has adequate capacity
- Is accessible to all residents (including elderly and disabled)
- Has necessary facilities (bathrooms, parking, shade, etc.)
- Is safe and secure

## Managing Event Participation

### Promoting Events

Maximize attendance by promoting through multiple channels:

1. **UniJAC Platform**: Events automatically appear on the neighborhood dashboard
2. **Announcements**: Create news posts highlighting upcoming events
3. **Word of Mouth**: Encourage JAC members to spread the word
4. **Physical Flyers**: Post in common areas for residents not using the app
5. **WhatsApp Groups**: Share event details in neighborhood chat groups

### Registration Management

For events requiring registration:

- Set capacity limits to match venue size
- Track registrations through the admin panel
- Send reminders to registered participants
- Keep a waitlist if the event fills up
- Follow up with no-shows to understand barriers to participation

### Volunteer Coordination

Many events need volunteer support:

1. Identify volunteer roles (setup, registration, activity leaders, cleanup)
2. Recruit volunteers from active community members
3. Assign clear responsibilities and schedules
4. Provide necessary training or materials
5. Recognize and thank volunteers publicly

## During the Event

### Day-of Checklist

- [ ] Arrive early to set up (at least 1 hour before start time)
- [ ] Verify all equipment and materials are present
- [ ] Set up registration or check-in table
- [ ] Place directional signage if needed
- [ ] Test audio/visual equipment
- [ ] Brief volunteers on their roles
- [ ] Have emergency contact numbers ready
- [ ] Designate someone to take photos for social media

### Tracking Attendance

Document participation for:
- Measuring event success
- Planning future events
- Reporting to municipal authorities or funders
- Recognizing active community members

### Safety Considerations

- Have a first aid kit available
- Identify the nearest medical facility
- Ensure adequate supervision for children's events
- Have a backup plan for outdoor events (weather)
- Maintain clear emergency exits

## After the Event

### Follow-Up Tasks

<Steps>
  <Step title="Thank Participants">
    Post a thank-you message on UniJAC and share event photos.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Update Event Status">
    Mark the event as "Completed" in the admin panel.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Collect Feedback">
    Survey participants about their experience and suggestions for improvement.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Document Results">
    Record attendance numbers, outcomes, and lessons learned.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Financial Reconciliation">
    If the event had a budget, account for all expenses and revenues.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Sharing Event Outcomes

Publish a post-event summary including:
- Number of participants
- Key highlights or achievements
- Photos from the event
- Thank yous to volunteers and sponsors
- Announcement of next similar event

This transparency shows the community that their participation matters and encourages attendance at future events.

## Managing the Events Calendar

### Viewing All Events

Residents can see upcoming events by:
1. Navigating to the **Eventos** section from the left sidebar
2. Viewing the "Eventos y actividades disponibles" widget on the main dashboard
3. Clicking "Ver más" to see the full events calendar

### Event Visibility

Events remain visible until:
- The event date passes
- You manually archive or delete the event
- The event reaches capacity (if registration is required)

### Recurring Events

For events that happen regularly (e.g., weekly rumba terapia):
1. Create the first event as normal
2. After completion, duplicate it for the next occurrence
3. Update only the date/time
4. Consider creating a series indicator (e.g., "Rumba terapia - Sesión 5")

## Event Metrics and Analytics

Track these metrics to improve your event planning:

- **Attendance Rate**: Percentage of registered participants who attended
- **Event Popularity**: Number of views or expressions of interest
- **Demographic Reach**: Age groups and household types participating
- **Frequency Preferences**: Which days/times have better turnout
- **Category Interest**: Which types of events attract most participants

### Using Metrics for Planning

Analyze patterns to:
- Schedule events at optimal times
- Focus on event types the community enjoys
- Identify underserved community segments
- Set realistic attendance goals
- Allocate resources efficiently

## Event Logistics Checklist

Use this comprehensive checklist for successful events:

### 3-4 Weeks Before
- [ ] Finalize event concept and goals
- [ ] Select date, time, and venue
- [ ] Obtain necessary permissions/permits
- [ ] Create event budget
- [ ] Create event in UniJAC system
- [ ] Begin promotion

### 1-2 Weeks Before
- [ ] Confirm venue reservation
- [ ] Order supplies and equipment
- [ ] Recruit and brief volunteers
- [ ] Send reminders to registered participants
- [ ] Coordinate with any guest speakers or performers
- [ ] Prepare registration materials

### 1-3 Days Before
- [ ] Final headcount and seating arrangements
- [ ] Pick up supplies
- [ ] Confirm volunteer attendance
- [ ] Check weather forecast (outdoor events)
- [ ] Prepare printed materials (schedules, handouts)
- [ ] Charge cameras and devices for documentation

### Day Of
- [ ] Arrive early for setup
- [ ] Test all equipment
- [ ] Set up registration area
- [ ] Welcome and brief volunteers
- [ ] Conduct the event
- [ ] Take photos/videos
- [ ] Clean up venue
- [ ] Thank participants and volunteers

### 1-3 Days After
- [ ] Share thank-you message and photos
- [ ] Update event status to completed
- [ ] Collect feedback
- [ ] Return rented equipment
- [ ] Complete financial accounting
- [ ] Document lessons learned

<Tip>
Create a digital or physical binder with checklists, vendor contacts, permit templates, and past event summaries. This becomes an invaluable resource for future event planning.
</Tip>

## Building Event Momentum

Successful event programs build over time:

### Start Small
Begin with simple, low-cost events to build confidence and community interest.

### Consistency
Regular events (monthly or quarterly) create expectations and habits.

### Variety
Rotate between event types to appeal to different community segments.

### Quality Over Quantity
A few well-executed events are better than many poorly planned ones.

### Community Input
Regularly ask residents what events they'd like to see.

### Celebrate Successes
Highlight positive outcomes and improvements resulting from community participation.

<Info>
Community events are more than entertainment—they build social capital, strengthen neighborhood identity, and create the connections that make communities resilient and sustainable.
</Info>
