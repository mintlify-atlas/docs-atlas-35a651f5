## Understanding Community Reports

Community members use UniJAC to report various issues affecting the neighborhood. As a JAC administrator, you're responsible for reviewing these reports, updating their status, and coordinating resolutions.

## Report Categories

UniJAC organizes reports into several categories visible in the left navigation panel:

### Daños (Infrastructure Damage)
Reports about damaged or non-functioning infrastructure:
- Street lights not working
- Damaged sidewalks or roads
- Broken public benches or fixtures
- Damaged traffic signals

**Example**: "Poste de luz no está funcionando" - A street light on manzana X is not illuminating, creating safety concerns.

### Extravíos (Lost Items/Pets)
Reports about lost pets, personal items, or found belongings that need to be returned to owners.

### Puntos Críticos (Critical Security Areas)
Areas identified as having security concerns:
- Locations with frequent robberies
- Poorly lit areas
- Suspicious activity reports

### Servicios Públicos (Public Services)
Issues with utilities and public services:
- Water service interruptions
- Power outages
- Gas service problems
- Telecommunications issues

### Espacio Público (Public Space)
Concerns about public spaces:
- Accumulated trash
- Illegal occupations
- Graffiti
- Damaged parks or recreation areas

## Accessing Reports

From your admin dashboard:

1. Use the left sidebar navigation buttons to filter by report category
2. Click on any category icon (Inicio, Eventos, Daños, Extravíos, Puntos críticos)
3. View the "Reportes de la comunidad" section in the main content area
4. Use the filter dropdown to show reports from the last 30 or 60 days

## Report Information

Each report card displays:

- **Report Title**: Brief description of the issue
- **Date Reported**: When the report was submitted (e.g., "Reportado el 31 de enero de 2026")
- **Reporter**: Username or "Vecino anónimo" for anonymous reports
- **Category**: Type of issue (e.g., "Daños de infraestructura")
- **Description**: Detailed explanation of the problem
- **Ver reporte button**: Click to view full report details

## Report Status Workflow

Manage reports through three key statuses:

### Pending (Pendiente)
Newly submitted reports awaiting JAC review.

**Actions to take**:
- Read the report carefully
- Verify the issue if possible
- Determine priority level
- Assign to appropriate personnel or department

### In Progress (En Progreso)
Reports being actively addressed.

**Actions to take**:
- Update the community on progress
- Coordinate with service providers or authorities
- Document steps being taken
- Set expected resolution timeframe

### Resolved (Resuelto)
Completed reports where the issue has been fixed.

**Actions to take**:
- Verify the issue is fully resolved
- Update the report with resolution details
- Thank the reporter for their participation
- Mark as resolved in the system

## Prioritizing Reports

Not all reports require the same urgency. Use this prioritization framework:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="High Priority" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    **Immediate attention required**
    - Safety hazards (broken street lights in high-traffic areas)
    - Major infrastructure failures affecting multiple residents
    - Public health concerns
    - Security threats
    
    **Response time**: Within 24 hours
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Medium Priority" icon="circle-exclamation">
    **Important but not immediate**
    - Minor infrastructure damage
    - Lost pets or valuable items
    - Service interruptions affecting small areas
    - Public space maintenance needs
    
    **Response time**: Within 3-5 days
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Low Priority" icon="circle-info">
    **Can be scheduled**
    - Cosmetic issues
    - General suggestions or ideas
    - Non-urgent maintenance
    - Informational reports
    
    **Response time**: Within 1-2 weeks
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Communicating with Reporters

Effective communication builds trust and encourages continued participation:

### Initial Acknowledgment
Respond to reports promptly to show the community their concerns are heard:

```text
"Gracias por reportar este problema. La JAC ha recibido su reporte 
y estamos evaluando la situación."
```

### Progress Updates
Keep reporters informed as you work on resolution:

```text
"Actualización: Hemos contactado a [service provider] y están 
programados para atender este problema el [date]."
```

### Resolution Notification
Inform the community when issues are resolved:

```text
"Resuelto: El poste de luz ha sido reparado. Gracias por su reporte 
y paciencia. Su participación ayuda a mejorar nuestro barrio."
```

## Best Practices for Report Management

### Daily Review
Check new reports at least once daily to ensure timely responses. Set a regular time (e.g., morning) for this task.

### Anonymous Reports
Reports marked "Vecino anónimo" still deserve the same attention and follow-up as identified reports.

### Document Everything
Keep detailed records of:
- Actions taken
- People or agencies contacted
- Dates and timelines
- Resolution outcomes

This documentation helps with accountability and future reference.

### Coordinate with Authorities
Many reports require coordination with:
- Municipal services (Alcaldía)
- Utility companies (water, electricity, gas)
- Police or security services
- Waste management services

Maintain contact information for these entities and establish working relationships.

### Group Similar Reports
If multiple residents report the same issue, group them together to:
- Avoid duplicate work
- Show the scope of the problem
- Provide mass updates efficiently

### Follow Up
After marking a report as resolved:
- Verify with the original reporter if possible
- Check physically that the issue is fixed
- Monitor for recurring problems

## Handling Challenging Reports

### Duplicate Reports
If multiple people report the same issue:
1. Link the reports together in your records
2. Update all reporters with the same information
3. Note in the primary report that it affects multiple residents

### Unclear Reports
If a report lacks sufficient detail:
1. Contact the reporter for clarification (if not anonymous)
2. Visit the location if possible
3. Ask other residents if they've observed the issue

### Outside JAC Authority
Some reports may be beyond JAC's direct control:
1. Acknowledge the report
2. Explain which authority is responsible
3. Offer to forward the report to the appropriate entity
4. Provide contact information for residents to follow up directly

### False or Spam Reports
Rarely, you may encounter inappropriate reports:
1. Document the report
2. Mark as resolved with a note about its nature
3. If patterns emerge, contact UniJAC support for assistance

## Building Community Trust

Effective report management strengthens the entire community:

- **Transparency**: Keep the community informed about what's being done
- **Responsiveness**: Reply to reports promptly, even if just to acknowledge receipt
- **Consistency**: Treat all reports with equal seriousness
- **Results**: Show tangible improvements from community participation
- **Appreciation**: Thank residents for taking time to report issues

<Info>
Remember: Every report represents a resident who cares enough about the neighborhood to speak up. Your response as a JAC administrator either encourages or discourages future participation.
</Info>
