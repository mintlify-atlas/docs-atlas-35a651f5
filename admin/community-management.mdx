## Overview of Community Management

As a JAC administrator, community management encompasses all activities related to maintaining active engagement, sharing information, and ensuring residents have access to the tools and knowledge they need to participate in neighborhood life.

## User Management

Managing your neighborhood's user base ensures the community remains safe, engaged, and well-organized.

### Verifying Residents

Maintain the integrity of your neighborhood community:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review Registration Requests">
    When new users register for your neighborhood, verify they are legitimate residents.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm Identity">
    Check that the provided address falls within your neighborhood boundaries.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Approve or Deny">
    Grant access to verified residents and deny suspicious or fraudulent requests.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Welcome New Members">
    Send a welcome message to approved users explaining how to use UniJAC effectively.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### User Roles and Permissions

UniJAC supports different user levels:

**Resident Users**:
- Submit reports
- View events and announcements
- Register for events
- Participate in community discussions

**JAC Administrators**:
- All resident permissions, plus:
- Manage reports (update status, respond)
- Create and manage events
- Publish announcements
- Manage neighborhood schedules
- View community analytics
- Moderate content

**Super Administrators**:
- All JAC admin permissions, plus:
- Grant/revoke admin access
- Manage other administrators
- Configure neighborhood settings
- Access advanced analytics

### Managing Active Users

Monitor and engage your community:

- **View User Statistics**: Track how many residents are registered and active
- **Identify Power Users**: Recognize residents who frequently report issues or attend events
- **Engagement Patterns**: Understand when residents are most active on the platform
- **Inactive Users**: Reach out to registered users who haven't engaged recently

### Handling Problem Users

Occasionally, you may need to address inappropriate behavior:

1. **Document Issues**: Keep records of policy violations
2. **Issue Warnings**: Contact users privately about concerns
3. **Temporary Suspension**: For repeated violations
4. **Permanent Ban**: Only for serious or repeated abuse
5. **Report to UniJAC**: Contact platform support for harassment or illegal activity

## Publishing Announcements and News

Keep your community informed through the "Noticias y novedades del sector" section.

### Types of Announcements

#### Critical Alerts
**Urgent information requiring immediate attention**:
- Security threats or warnings
- Emergency service interruptions (water, power)
- Natural disaster preparations
- Public health emergencies
- Road closures or traffic changes

**Format**: Bold title with urgency indicator, brief description, and action steps.

#### Schedule Changes
**Updates to regular neighborhood activities**:
- Changes to garbage collection schedules
- Temporary road work or maintenance
- Service provider schedule adjustments
- Event postponements or cancellations

**Format**: Clear title, old vs. new schedule, and dates affected.

#### General News
**Information of community interest**:
- New local businesses
- Municipal improvements or projects
- Community achievements or recognition
- Seasonal information
- JAC meeting summaries

**Format**: Informative title, detailed description, and any relevant links or contacts.

#### Reminders
**Recurring or upcoming information**:
- Upcoming event reminders
- Garbage collection day reminders
- Payment deadlines (community fees, utilities)
- Seasonal tips (rainy season preparations, holiday safety)

**Format**: Friendly tone, key dates highlighted, and links to more information.

### Creating an Announcement

From the admin dashboard:

1. Navigate to the **Announcements** section
2. Click "Create New Announcement"
3. Fill in the announcement form:

**Required Fields**:
- **Title**: Clear, concise headline (e.g., "Cambio en horario de recolección de basura")
- **Content**: Full announcement text with all relevant details
- **Priority Level**: Critical, High, Normal, or Low
- **Publication Date**: When the announcement should appear

**Optional Fields**:
- **Expiration Date**: When to automatically remove the announcement
- **Category**: Type of news (safety, schedule, general, event)
- **Attachments**: Documents or images to support the announcement
- **Target Audience**: All residents or specific groups

4. Preview the announcement to ensure proper formatting
5. Click "Publish" to make it visible on the neighborhood dashboard

### Announcement Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Write Clear, Concise Titles">
    Use descriptive titles that immediately convey the announcement's purpose. Good: "Servicio de agua suspendido 15/03 - 9 AM a 2 PM". Bad: "Aviso importante".
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Lead with Key Information">
    Put the most important details in the first sentence. Residents should understand the announcement's impact within seconds.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Include Specific Dates and Times">
    Always use the format DD/MM/YYYY for dates and 12-hour format for times (e.g., "14/03/2026 – 7:00 AM").
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Provide Action Steps">
    Tell residents what they should do in response to the announcement, if anything.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Maintain a Respectful Tone">
    Use formal but friendly language. Address residents as "vecinos" or "comunidad".
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Proofread Before Publishing">
    Check for spelling, grammar, and factual accuracy. Errors undermine credibility.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Managing Published Announcements

- **Edit Announcements**: Update information if details change
- **Pin Important Items**: Keep critical announcements at the top of the feed
- **Archive Old News**: Remove outdated announcements to keep the feed relevant
- **Track Engagement**: Monitor how many residents view each announcement

## Managing Neighborhood Schedules

Maintain important recurring schedules that residents rely on daily.

### Garbage Collection Schedule

The most commonly referenced schedule in neighborhoods:

**Current Example from Dashboard**:
- **Days**: Lun (Monday), Mie (Wednesday), Vie (Friday)
- **Time**: 7:00 PM

#### Updating the Garbage Schedule

1. Navigate to **Schedule Management** in the admin panel
2. Select "Recolección de basura"
3. Update collection days by checking the appropriate boxes:
   - Lun (Monday)
   - Mar (Tuesday)
   - Mie (Wednesday)
   - Jue (Thursday)
   - Vie (Friday)
   - Sab (Saturday)
   - Dom (Sunday)
4. Set the collection time using the time picker
5. Save changes

The updated schedule will immediately appear in the "Horarios importantes" section of the neighborhood dashboard.

<Note>
When garbage collection schedules change, also publish an announcement to ensure residents are aware of the update.
</Note>

### Other Important Schedules

Consider maintaining schedules for:

**Utility Services**:
- Water service interruptions (planned maintenance)
- Power company maintenance windows
- Gas service schedules

**Community Services**:
- JAC office hours
- Security patrol schedules
- Common area maintenance
- Cleaning service schedules

**Seasonal Schedules**:
- Holiday-related service changes
- School schedules affecting neighborhood traffic
- Rainy season drainage checks

### Schedule Display Format

Schedules appear in the sidebar of the neighborhood dashboard in a consistent format:

```text
Horarios importantes

📅 Recolección de basura
└─ Lun, Mie, Vie
└─ 7:00 PM
```

Keep schedule information:
- **Concise**: Only essential information
- **Accurate**: Verify with service providers before posting
- **Updated**: Review and refresh regularly

## Community Engagement Tools

Active engagement builds a stronger, more resilient neighborhood.

### Encouraging Participation

Strategies to increase resident involvement:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Regular Communication" icon="megaphone">
    Post announcements weekly, even if just to share good news or community appreciation.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Recognize Contributors" icon="trophy">
    Publicly thank residents who report issues, volunteer, or attend events.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Share Success Stories" icon="star">
    Highlight problems that were solved because of community reports.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Make It Easy" icon="hand-pointer">
    Simplify the reporting and participation process. Provide instructions for new users.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Measuring Engagement

Track these metrics to understand community participation:

**Platform Activity**:
- Number of active users (logged in past 30 days)
- Reports submitted per month
- Event registrations and attendance
- Announcement views

**Community Health**:
- Response rate to reports
- Time to resolve issues
- Event attendance trends
- New user growth

**Engagement Quality**:
- Repeat participants (users who regularly engage)
- Volunteer recruitment success
- Positive feedback or testimonials
- Reduced complaints about unresolved issues

### Building Community Spirit

Beyond just managing logistics, foster a sense of community:

**Celebrate Milestones**:
- Neighborhood anniversaries
- Achievement of community goals (X reports resolved, Y events held)
- Recognition of long-time residents or volunteer leaders

**Share Community Stories**:
- Profiles of interesting residents
- History of the neighborhood
- Local businesses and entrepreneurs
- Student achievements

**Encourage Connection**:
- Organize ice-breaker events
- Create opportunities for neighbors to meet
- Facilitate skill-sharing (one resident teaches another)
- Support neighborhood traditions

### Conflict Resolution

Occasionally, disputes arise between community members:

1. **Stay Neutral**: As a JAC administrator, you represent all residents equally
2. **Listen to All Sides**: Understand each perspective before taking action
3. **Focus on Solutions**: Help parties find mutually acceptable resolutions
4. **Document Everything**: Keep records of complaints and resolutions
5. **Escalate When Needed**: Some issues require police or legal intervention
6. **Maintain Privacy**: Don't share details of disputes publicly

## Communication Channels

While UniJAC is the primary platform, effective JAC administrators use multiple channels:

### Digital Channels
- **UniJAC Platform**: Official announcements and event organization
- **WhatsApp Groups**: Quick updates and informal communication
- **Facebook Pages**: Broader reach for announcements and photos
- **Email Lists**: Formal communications and meeting notices

### Physical Channels
- **Bulletin Boards**: Posted in common areas for non-digital residents
- **Printed Flyers**: Distributed door-to-door for major events
- **Community Meetings**: Face-to-face discussions for important decisions
- **JAC Office Hours**: In-person availability for questions and concerns

### Channel Strategy

Use the right channel for each message type:

| Message Type | Primary Channel | Secondary Channel |
|--------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Critical Alerts | UniJAC + WhatsApp | Physical flyers |
| Event Announcements | UniJAC Events | Facebook + Flyers |
| Schedule Changes | UniJAC Announcements | WhatsApp |
| Policy Changes | Email + UniJAC | Community Meeting |
| Reminders | WhatsApp | UniJAC |
| Detailed Information | UniJAC | Email |

## Community Data and Privacy

As an administrator with access to community data, you have important responsibilities:

### Data Protection
- **Never Share Personal Information**: Resident addresses, phone numbers, or personal details are confidential
- **Secure Your Account**: Use strong passwords and never share login credentials
- **Respect Anonymous Reports**: Don't try to identify anonymous reporters
- **GDPR Compliance**: If applicable, follow data protection regulations

### Transparency
- **Explain Data Use**: Be clear about what data is collected and why
- **Report Statistics Only**: Share aggregate data, not individual information
- **Give Users Control**: Allow residents to update or delete their information

## Best Practices for Community Management

  <Accordion title="Consistency is Key">

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Be Proactive, Not Reactive">
    Don't wait for problems to arise. Share positive news, organize preventive initiatives, and build goodwill during calm periods.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Lead by Example">
    Your behavior as a JAC administrator sets the tone. Be respectful, responsive, and engaged to encourage others to do the same.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Collaborate with Other Leaders">
    Work with other JAC members, municipal officials, and community organizations. No single person can manage a community alone.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Continuous Improvement">
    Regularly solicit feedback from residents about how the JAC and UniJAC platform can better serve the community.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Celebrate Progress">
    Acknowledge improvements, no matter how small. Show residents that their participation makes a difference.
  </Accordion>

## Monthly Community Management Checklist

Use this checklist to ensure comprehensive community management:

### Week 1
- [ ] Review and respond to all pending reports
- [ ] Post monthly newsletter/announcement summarizing previous month
- [ ] Check and update all schedules
- [ ] Plan events for upcoming 4-6 weeks
- [ ] Review user registration requests

### Week 2
- [ ] Analyze engagement metrics from previous month
- [ ] Reach out to inactive users
- [ ] Coordinate with service providers on any ongoing issues
- [ ] Prepare materials for upcoming events
- [ ] Update any outdated information on the platform

### Week 3
- [ ] Publish reminders for upcoming events
- [ ] Review and archive old announcements
- [ ] Check in with other JAC administrators
- [ ] Address any emerging community concerns
- [ ] Plan content for next month

### Week 4
- [ ] Conduct any scheduled community events
- [ ] Send thank-you messages to volunteers and participants
- [ ] Document lessons learned
- [ ] Prepare monthly report for JAC leadership
- [ ] Set goals for the following month
