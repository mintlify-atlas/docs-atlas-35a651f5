## What Sanitation Issues Can You Report?

The sanitation category, labeled "Aseo, basuras y puntos críticos" in UniJAC, helps communities maintain clean and healthy public spaces. You can report:

- **Basuras domiciliarias** - Household trash left in unauthorized locations on sidewalks or street corners
- **Muebles viejos** - Old furniture, tires, grass clippings, or yard waste dumped in public spaces
- **Botaderos ilegales** - Illegal dumping sites near rivers, parks, or green areas that damage the environment
- **Malos olores** - Sites with bad odors and/or presence of rodents and flies
- **Puntos críticos** - Critical accumulation points that require immediate attention

<Warning>
Sanitation issues don't just affect aesthetics - they can pose serious health risks through disease transmission, attract pests, and contaminate water sources.
</Warning>

## How to Submit a Sanitation Report

The sanitation reporting system includes advanced features for tracking and mapping issues:

### Report Information to Include

1. **Specific location** - Street address or notable landmark
2. **Type of issue** - Select from the categories above
3. **Description** - Detail what waste materials are present
4. **Photos** - Visual evidence of the sanitation problem
5. **Urgency level** - Based on health risk and severity

## Interactive Map System

The sanitation reports page includes an interactive map with color-coded markers:

<Info>
**Map Legend:**
- 🟠 **Orange dot** - Basuras domiciliarias en andenes/esquina en lugares no autorizados
- 🟣 **Purple dot** - Muebles viejos, llantas, poda/cesped en espacios publicos
- 🔵 **Blue dot** - Botaderos cerca de ríos, parques zonas verdes que deterioran el entorno
- 🟢 **Green dot** - Sitios con malos olores y presencia de roedores/moscas
</Info>

The map allows you to:
- See all sanitation reports in your area at once
- Click markers to view report details
- Identify patterns and critical areas
- Navigate to specific problem zones

<Tip>
Place garbage out only on collection days to avoid creating sanitation issues. Early or late placement attracts animals and pests.
</Tip>

## Status Tracking System

Sanitation reports use a badge system to show resolution status:

- **Sin resolver** (Unresolved) - Report submitted, awaiting action
- **En proceso** (In progress) - Clean-up scheduled or underway
- **Resuelto** (Resolved) - Issue has been addressed

## What Happens After You Report?

<Steps>
  <Step title="Immediate posting">
    Your report appears in the "Últimos reportes" list with timestamp
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map marker added">
    A color-coded marker appears on the interactive map at the reported location
  </Step>
  <Step title="Community visibility">
    All residents can see the report and add supporting information
  </Step>
  <Step title="JAC prioritization">
    Community leaders review and prioritize based on health risk and severity
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resolution coordination">
    JAC coordinates with sanitation services or volunteers for clean-up
  </Step>
  <Step title="Status update">
    Report status updates as work progresses
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Environmental Impact

Reporting illegal dumping sites is especially important for protecting:

- **Ríos** (Rivers) - Dumping near water sources contaminates drinking water
- **Parques** (Parks) - Maintains green spaces for community recreation
- **Zonas verdes** (Green areas) - Preserves natural areas and biodiversity

<Note>
UniJAC's sanitation tracking helps neighborhoods work toward UN Sustainable Development Goal 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities.
</Note>

## Tips for Effective Sanitation Reports

1. **Be specific about location** - Include cross streets or landmarks
2. **Document the extent** - Show how much waste has accumulated
3. **Note duration** - Mention if this is a recurring problem
4. **Include safety concerns** - Report any hazardous materials
5. **Suggest solutions** - If you know what resources are needed

## Common Sanitation Issues

| Issue Type | Common Locations | Prevention |
|------------|------------------|------------|
| Household trash | Esquinas (corners), sidewalks | Follow collection schedule |
| Furniture dumping | Empty lots, parks | Use authorized disposal services |
| Illegal dumping | Near rivers, isolated areas | Community monitoring |
| Bad odors/pests | Accumulation points | Regular cleaning, proper disposal |

<Warning>
If you encounter hazardous waste (chemicals, medical waste, etc.), report it immediately and mark as critical severity.
</Warning>
