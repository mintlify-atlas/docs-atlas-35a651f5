## What Security Issues Can You Report?

The security category ("Seguridad y convivencia") helps neighbors stay informed about safety concerns in their community. This report type covers:

- **Robos** (Robberies) - Theft incidents affecting residents or properties
- **Extravíos** (Lost items) - Missing personal belongings or items of value
- **Hurtos** (Thefts) - Petty theft or pickpocketing incidents
- **Personas sospechosas** (Suspicious persons) - Unusual activity or concerning behavior
- **Convivencia** (Community living issues) - Neighbor disputes or coexistence concerns
- **Safety concerns** - Poorly lit areas, unsafe conditions, or security vulnerabilities

<Warning>
**Important:** For emergencies or crimes in progress, always call local authorities first (Policía Nacional 123). Use UniJAC reports for documentation and community awareness.
</Warning>

## How to Submit a Security Report

When reporting security concerns, include:

### Essential Information

1. **Date and time** - When the incident occurred
2. **Exact location** - Street address, intersection, or landmark
3. **Type of incident** - Select appropriate category (robo, hurto, etc.)
4. **Description** - What happened, who was involved, what was taken
5. **Police report number** - If authorities were contacted
6. **Safety recommendations** - Advice for neighbors

### Sample Security Report

```javascript Example Report
{
  "category": "Seguridad",
  "subcategory": "Robo",
  "date": "2026-02-15T22:30",
  "location": "Calle 80 con Carrera 15",
  "description": "Robo de celular en la vía pública. Dos personas en motocicleta.",
  "policeReport": "CAI-2026-001234",
  "recommendations": "Evitar uso de celular en la calle, especialmente después de las 8 PM",
  "status": "Reportado a autoridades"
}
```

<Tip>
Be factual and objective in your descriptions. Avoid speculation and focus on observable details that can help identify patterns or suspects.
</Tip>

## Security Report Categories Explained

### Robos (Robberies)

Robberies typically involve:
- Direct confrontation or threats
- Use of force or weapons
- Loss of personal belongings or valuables
- Vehicle-related thefts

**What to report:**
- Time and location of incident
- Number of perpetrators and descriptions
- Method used (motorcycle, on foot, etc.)
- Items taken
- Direction suspects fled

### Extravíos (Lost Items)

Distinct from theft, these reports document:
- Items lost by owners (not stolen)
- Missing personal belongings
- Potentially found items awaiting owners

<Note>
Some lost items may be better reported in the [Lost and Found](/reports/lost-and-found) category, especially personal documents, keys, or pets.
</Note>

### Hurtos (Thefts)

Theft without direct confrontation:
- Pickpocketing
- Items stolen from vehicles
- Theft from homes or businesses
- Opportunistic thefts

### Personas Sospechosas (Suspicious Activity)

Report concerning behavior such as:
- Unknown persons surveying properties
- Suspicious vehicles parked for extended periods
- Attempts to access locked areas
- Unusual activity at odd hours

<Warning>
Never confront suspicious persons yourself. Report observations to authorities and document on UniJAC for community awareness.
</Warning>

## Privacy and Safety Considerations

<Steps>
  <Step title="Protect victim privacy">
    Don't include full names, addresses, or identifying details of victims
  </Step>
  <Step title="Avoid vigilantism">
    Reports are for awareness, not for organizing confrontations or retaliation
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify information">
    Only report incidents you witnessed or can confirm from reliable sources
  </Step>
  <Step title="Coordinate with authorities">
    Share serious incidents with police and reference official report numbers
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Happens After You Report?

1. **Immediate notification** - Report posts to the security feed visible to all neighborhood residents
2. **JAC review** - Community leaders assess the severity and patterns
3. **Alert distribution** - Critical incidents may trigger community-wide alerts
4. **Coordination with authorities** - JAC may contact local police with aggregated data
5. **Pattern analysis** - Multiple reports help identify crime hotspots
6. **Security measures** - Community may organize patrols or request security improvements

## Preventive Security Measures

Based on community reports, JAC can:

- Request additional street lighting in high-risk areas
- Organize neighborhood watch programs
- Install security cameras in common areas
- Coordinate with police for increased patrols
- Conduct security awareness workshops

<Info>
Regular security reporting helps your JAC demonstrate need when requesting resources from local government for safety improvements.
</Info>

## Best Practices for Security Reports

### Do:
- Report incidents promptly while details are fresh
- Include specific times, locations, and descriptions
- Reference police report numbers when available
- Provide actionable safety recommendations
- Update reports if suspects are apprehended

### Don't:
- Post identifying information about victims
- Share unverified rumors or speculation
- Use reports to accuse specific individuals
- Include personal contact information publicly
- Delay contacting authorities for serious crimes

## Integration with Other Features
