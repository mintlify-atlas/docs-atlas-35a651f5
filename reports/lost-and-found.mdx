## What Can You Report in Lost and Found?

The lost and found category, labeled "Desorientación y objetos" in UniJAC, helps community members recover lost items and return found objects to their owners. You can report:

- **Documentos** (Documents) - ID cards, passports, licenses, certificates
- **Llaves** (Keys) - House keys, car keys, office keys
- **Dispositivos electrónicos** (Electronic devices) - Phones, tablets, laptops
- **Objetos personales** (Personal items) - Wallets, bags, jewelry, clothing
- **Mascotas** (Pets) - Lost or found dogs, cats, and other animals
- **Artículos de valor** (Valuables) - Money, important documents, sentimental items

<Info>
This category facilitates community assistance in reuniting people with their belongings, strengthening neighborhood bonds through helping each other.
</Info>

## Lost vs. Found: Two Types of Reports

### Reporting Lost Items

When you've lost something:

1. **Detailed description** - Color, brand, distinctive features
2. **Where lost** - Last known location or area
3. **When lost** - Date and approximate time
4. **Contact information** - How finders can reach you
5. **Reward** (optional) - If offering a reward for return

### Reporting Found Items

When you've found something:

1. **Item description** - What you found (avoid excessive details to verify ownership)
2. **Where found** - Location where item was discovered
3. **When found** - Date and time
4. **Safe keeping location** - Where the item is being held
5. **Verification questions** - Ask claimants to describe specific details

<Tip>
For found items, don't include all identifying details in your public post. Ask claimants to describe specific features to verify ownership.
</Tip>

## Found Item Safety Locations

Common safe places to hold found items:

- **JAC office** - Community action office
- **TransMilenio stations** - As shown in the example report
- **Local police station** (CAI) - For valuable items
- **Neighborhood businesses** - Trusted local shops
- **Religious centers** - Churches or community centers
- **Schools** - For items found near educational institutions

<Warning>
**For valuable items or documents:** Consider taking them directly to a police station (CAI) to ensure proper handling and create an official record.
</Warning>

## Understanding "Desorientación"

The Spanish term "desorientación" (disorientation) captures the broader concept:

- Items that are "disoriented" (lost) from their owners
- People who may be disoriented (lost persons, elderly wandering)
- Objects out of place that need to be reunited with owners

This holistic approach emphasizes community care and looking out for each other.

## What Happens After You Report?

### For Lost Items

1. **Report posts** to community feed
2. **Neighbors see it** and check if they've seen or found the item
3. **Responses come in** from potential finders
4. **Verification** through specific questions about the item
5. **Reunion arranged** between owner and finder
6. **Status updated** to "Recuperado" when returned

### For Found Items

1. **Report posts** with general description
2. **Potential owners respond** with detailed descriptions
3. **Verification questions** confirm true ownership
4. **Meeting arranged** at safe location (JAC office, police station)
5. **Item returned** to rightful owner
6. **Report marked** as "Recuperado"

## Best Practices for Lost Item Reports

### Do:
- Report as soon as possible while item might still be nearby
- Provide clear, detailed descriptions
- Include photos if you have them (especially for pets)
- Check back frequently for responses
- Update your report with new information
- Thank finders publicly to encourage community participation

### Don't:
- Include extremely valuable items' full details publicly (contact police)
- Give personal address in initial report
- Meet unknown people in isolated locations
- Claim items without being able to verify ownership

## Best Practices for Found Item Reports

### Do:
- Report found items promptly
- Keep items secure and safe
- Ask verification questions before returning
- Use public or official locations for returns
- Take photos for your records
- Mark report as resolved once returned

### Don't:
- Include every identifying detail (save some for verification)
- Keep valuable items at home for extended periods
- Return items without proper verification
- Expect rewards (but graciously accept if offered)

## Document Handling

Special considerations for found documents:

  - Take to nearest police station (CAI) or TransMilenio station
  - Don't post photos showing full ID numbers or addresses
  - Report that documents were found and where they're being held
  
  **Official Documents:**
  - Contact issuing institution if possible
  - Hold securely until claimed
  - Require proper identification before returning
</Card>

## Privacy and Safety

<Warning>
**Privacy Considerations:**
- Don't post personal addresses publicly
- Use UniJAC messaging for detailed communication
- Meet in public places (JAC office, police station, busy cafes)
- For valuable items, require identification verification
- Keep records of returns (photos, witnesses)
</Warning>

## Beyond Objects: Community Care

The "desorientación" category can extend to:

- **Lost children** - Helping kids find parents (always involve authorities)
- **Elderly persons** - Assisting disoriented seniors return home
- **Tourists** - Helping visitors who are lost
- **Emergency situations** - Coordinating help for those in need

<Note>
For any situation involving vulnerable persons (children, elderly, disabled), immediately contact authorities (Policía 123) while also posting to UniJAC for community awareness.
</Note>

## Tips for Increasing Success

1. **Post quickly** - The sooner you report, the better the chances
2. **Be descriptive** - Include unique features and distinctive marks
3. **Use multiple channels** - Post on UniJAC and also check with neighbors
4. **Check regularly** - Monitor your report for responses
5. **Share widely** - Ask neighbors to share your report
6. **Follow up** - Update your report with outcome
