## What Infrastructure Issues Can You Report?

The infrastructure report category helps residents document and track physical damage to public spaces and roads in their neighborhood. This system allows the community to:

- **Deterioro vial** (Road deterioration) - potholes, cracked asphalt, damaged roads
- **Damaged sidewalks** - broken or uneven sidewalks that pose safety risks
- **Street lighting issues** - malfunctioning or broken street lights
- **Damaged public furniture** - broken benches, bus stops, or signs
- **Drainage problems** - clogged drains or flooding issues

<Info>
Infrastructure reports help your JAC (Junta de Acción Comunal) prioritize repairs and communicate with local authorities about maintenance needs.
</Info>

## How to Submit an Infrastructure Report

When reporting infrastructure damage, include the following information:

1. **Location** - Specific street address or intersection
2. **Type of damage** - Select from categories like deterioro vial, sidewalk damage, etc.
3. **Severity level** - Indicate if it's minor, moderate, or severe
4. **Photo evidence** - Attach clear photos showing the damage
5. **Description** - Brief explanation of the issue and any safety concerns

## What Happens After You Submit?

Once you submit an infrastructure report:

1. **Immediate visibility** - Your report appears in the "Casos reportados" (Reported Cases) table
2. **Community awareness** - Neighbors can see the issue and add their observations
3. **JAC review** - Local community leaders review and prioritize the report
4. **Status tracking** - Monitor your report status: Pendiente (Pending), En proceso (In progress), or Resuelto (Resolved)
5. **Authority notification** - JAC communicates with local government for repairs

## Best Practices for Infrastructure Reports

<Steps>
  <Step title="Take clear photos">
    Capture the damage from multiple angles showing the full extent and any reference points
  </Step>
  <Step title="Note exact location">
    Include street names, nearby landmarks, or GPS coordinates
  </Step>
  <Step title="Describe safety impact">
    Explain how the damage affects pedestrians, vehicles, or accessibility
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check for duplicates">
    Review existing reports to avoid duplicate submissions for the same issue
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Infrastructure issues often affect entire streets or blocks. Check if neighbors have already reported the same problem and add your observations to existing reports.
</Tip>

## Common Infrastructure Issues in Bogotá Neighborhoods

- **Potholes** - Especially common after rainy seasons
- **Cracked sidewalks** - Caused by tree roots or weather damage
- **Broken street lights** - Affecting neighborhood safety
- **Drainage issues** - Leading to flooding during rain
- **Damaged speed bumps** - Creating traffic hazards

<Note>
Your infrastructure reports help create a documented history of maintenance needs, which JAC leaders can use when requesting government resources and prioritizing community improvements.
</Note>
