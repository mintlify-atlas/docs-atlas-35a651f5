## What Public Service Issues Can You Report?

The public services category ("Servicios públicos") helps residents identify whether service disruptions are isolated to their home or affecting the entire neighborhood. Report issues with:

- **Agua** (Water) - No water supply, low pressure, or water quality issues
- **Electricidad** (Electricity) - Power outages, voltage fluctuations, or electrical failures
- **Gas natural** (Natural gas) - Gas supply interruptions or pressure problems
- **Internet/Teléfono** (Internet/Phone) - Communication service disruptions
- **Alcantarillado** (Sewage) - Drainage or sewage system problems
- **Alumbrado público** (Street lighting) - Non-functioning public lighting

<Info>
Public services reports help you quickly determine if an outage is affecting only your home (requiring you to contact your provider) or if it's a neighborhood-wide issue requiring collective action.
</Info>

## How to Submit a Public Services Report

When reporting a service disruption, include:

### Essential Information

1. **Service type** - Water, electricity, gas, etc.
2. **Time started** - When you first noticed the issue
3. **Location** - Specific streets or blocks affected
4. **Description** - Nature of the problem (complete outage, intermittent, quality issue)
5. **Provider contacted** - Whether you've called your service provider
6. **Ticket number** - If you received a service request number

### Sample Public Services Report

```javascript Example Report
{
  "category": "Servicios Públicos",

```

<Tip>
Always contact your service provider first for outages, then post to UniJAC to help neighbors understand the situation and expected resolution time.
</Tip>

## Service Categories Explained

### Fallas de Electricidad (Electrical Failures)

**Common issues:**
- Complete power outages (cortes de luz)
- Voltage fluctuations affecting appliances
- Partial outages (some circuits working)
- Transformer failures

**When to report:**
- If multiple homes are affected
- For extended outages (>2 hours)
- For frequent recurring outages
- When provider estimates are delayed

### Fallas de Agua (Water Service Issues)

**Common issues:**
- Complete water supply interruption
- Low water pressure
- Discolored or cloudy water
- Scheduled maintenance periods

**When to report:**
- Unannounced water cuts
- Water quality concerns
- Extended service interruptions
- When multiple households are affected

### Fallas de Gas Natural (Gas Service Issues)

**Common issues:**
- No gas supply to homes
- Low gas pressure affecting appliances
- Gas odors or suspected leaks

<Warning>
**Gas Safety:** If you smell gas, evacuate immediately, don't use electrical switches or phones indoors, and call emergency services (123) and your gas provider from a safe distance.
</Warning>

### Alcantarillado y Drenaje (Sewage and Drainage)

**Common issues:**
- Backed up sewage systems
- Overflowing manholes
- Drainage problems causing flooding
- Bad odors from drains

<Note>
Sewage issues often overlap with infrastructure reports. If the problem is damaged pipes or infrastructure, consider reporting in [Infrastructure Reports](/reports/infrastructure) as well.
</Note>

## Scheduled vs. Unscheduled Outages

### Scheduled Maintenance

Service providers sometimes schedule maintenance:
- Usually announced 48-72 hours in advance
- Typically during low-usage hours
- Have defined start and end times

**Action:** Post scheduled maintenance notices on UniJAC so neighbors can prepare (fill water containers, plan around power needs, etc.)

### Unscheduled Outages

Unexpected service interruptions:
- Emergency repairs
- Equipment failures
- Weather-related damage
- Accidents affecting infrastructure

**Action:** Report immediately so neighbors aren't surprised and can take appropriate action.

## Status Tracking for Service Reports

Public services reports use status indicators:

- **Reportado** (Reported) - Issue confirmed, provider contacted
- **Confirmado por proveedor** (Confirmed by provider) - Provider acknowledged the issue
- **Reparación en curso** (Repair in progress) - Technicians are working on it
- **Servicio restaurado** (Service restored) - Issue resolved
- **Pendiente** (Pending) - Awaiting provider response

## Documentation for Advocacy

Frequent service disruptions? UniJAC reports create documentation:

1. **Track frequency** - How often outages occur
2. **Duration records** - How long services are down
3. **Pattern identification** - Time of day, days of week, seasonal trends
4. **Impact assessment** - Number of households affected

<Info>
This documentation helps your JAC formally request service improvements, file complaints with regulatory agencies, or negotiate better service agreements.
</Info>

## Best Practices for Service Reports

### Be Specific
- Include exact times when service was lost and restored
- Specify which streets or blocks are affected
- Note any patterns (e.g., "outages every Tuesday evening")

### Update Regularly
- Edit your report when you get new information
- Share provider responses or ticket numbers
- Confirm when service is restored

### Coordinate with Neighbors
- Check if others have the same issue before calling provider
- Share provider responses with the community
- Collaborate on filing complaints if service is consistently poor

### Include Context
- Note if weather might be a factor
- Mention if you saw repair trucks or crews
- Share any official communications from providers

## Emergency Service Disruptions

Some service disruptions require immediate action:

<Warning>
**Immediate Emergencies:**
- Gas leaks - evacuate and call 164 or 123
- Downed power lines - stay away and call 115
- Sewage backups into homes - call 116
- Widespread flooding - call 123

Report these to authorities FIRST, then document on UniJAC.
</Warning>

## Integration with Other Reports

Public service issues often relate to other report types:

- **Infrastructure** - Damaged infrastructure causing service disruptions
- **Sanitation** - Sewage backups create cleanliness issues
- **Security** - Non-functioning street lights create safety concerns
